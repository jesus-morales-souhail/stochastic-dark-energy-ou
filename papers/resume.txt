1. BAO ANALYSIS — ORNSTEIN–UHLENBECK + QNM MODELS (DESI DR2)
================================================================================

Data: DESI DR2 BAO (arXiv:2503.14738) — 7 redshift bins
      Public summary statistics; diagonal measurement errors in this pipeline.

Background (CPL, BAO-only):
  w0 ≈ −0.99
  wa ≈ −0.02
  (close to flat ΛCDM under the fiducial used for α = 1)

Models tested on residuals:
- Baseline: ΛCDM (diagonal covariance only)
- H0: Stationary Ornstein–Uhlenbeck (OU) fluctuations
- H1: Damped oscillatory QNM kernel

Results:

  OU model (H0):
    θ     = 0.001  (≈ boundary, effectively undamped)
    σ_X   = 5 × 10⁻⁵
    Δlogℒ vs ΛCDM = 0.00
    AIC = -50.03 | BIC = -50.14

  QNM model (H1):
    θ     = 0.001
    σ_X   = 5 × 10⁻⁵
    ω_R   = 0.00
    Δlogℒ vs ΛCDM = 0.00
    AIC = -48.03 | BIC = -48.19

Conclusion:
- No statistical evidence for stochastic fluctuations in the dark energy sector.
- The BAO summary statistics are consistent with a smooth background near ΛCDM.
- 95% CL upper limit: σ_X < 1.5 × 10⁻⁴
- The stochastic amplitude is driven to zero when the background is free.
  Nested CPL + (σ, θ) extensions are not preferred (ΔAIC ≈ +4).

2. ANGULAR CROSS-CORRELATION TEST (DESI DR1 + Pantheon+)
================================================================================

Data used:
- DESI DR1 LRG clustering catalogs (NGC + SGC)
- Pantheon+SH0ES supernovae (1701 objects)

Sample:
- Total galaxies: 1,278,805
- Overlap pixels: 67
- Measured correlation: r = 0.1673 ± 0.0613 (Z ≈ 2.73σ)
- OU model prediction: r ≈ 0.0227

Status: Preliminary
Note: Systematics not fully controlled. A reanalysis with DESI DR2 (including the WEIGHT_SYS column) is required for robust conclusions.

================================================================================
3. STOCHASTIC DARK ENERGY MODEL STATUS
================================================================================

Main result:
The stationary stochastic model (Ornstein–Uhlenbeck + QNM) is **not favored** by DESI DR2 BAO data.

- When marginalizing over the background cosmology, the stochastic amplitude is consistent with zero.
- This constitutes a **strict upper limit**, not a detection.
- The result supports the working hypothesis of **Late-Time Vacuum Homogeneity** at currently observable scales.

Future tests:
- Euclid DR1 (expected H2 2026) will provide >20 BAO bins, offering significantly higher sensitivity to fluctuations at the level of σ_X > 10⁻⁵.
- The current analysis pipeline is ready to be applied to upcoming datasets.
