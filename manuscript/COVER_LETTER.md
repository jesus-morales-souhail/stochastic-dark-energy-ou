**Re:** Constraints on Stochastic Dark Energy from DESI DR2 BAO: A Null Result and the \(10^{56}\) Amplification Bottleneck 
**Author:** Jesús Morales Souhail (ORCID 0009-0000-7637-1818)

Dear Editor,

Please find enclosed a manuscript reporting constraints on *stationary stochastic* dark-energy residuals using public DESI DR2 BAO summary statistics, together with a controlled exclusion of a coherent tachyonic growth model and a quantitative audit of the amplification gap between motivational Planck-scale seeds and BAO residual amplitudes.

The main results are **null and exclusionary**: the data prefer smooth residual evolution (\(\sigma_X < 1.5\times 10^{-4}\) at 95% CL under a stated OU kernel); a rank-1 coherent growing mode is strongly disfavoured (\(\Delta\chi^2\approx +22.7\)); and we show that linear unphysical free amplifiers of a Sorkin-type seed fail by many decades, with machine scans of three open cards documenting their theoretical price.

The work is intentionally conservative: we separate data constraints from geometric interpretation, exclude preliminary cross-correlation tests from the primary claim set, and isolate laboratory-scale optical digressions in a separate exploratory repository so that referees can review a clean cosmological claim spine.

All analysis code and numerical tables are public (MIT). I hope the combination of a clear null result, a model exclusion with the correct covariance, and an honest amplification map will be of interest to your readers.
